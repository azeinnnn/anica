Planetary Registry

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in our solar system. It lacks a significant atmosphere, causing extreme temperature swings. It orbits the Sun every 88 Earth days.

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system due to its thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect. It rotates in the opposite direction of most planets.

Earth

Our home is the only planet known to harbor life. With its vast oceans and oxygen-rich atmosphere, Earth is a rare jewel in the cosmic desert.

Mars

The Red Planet is home to the largest volcano in the solar system. It is a cold, desert world where scientists look for signs of ancient water and life.

Jupiter

Jupiter is a gas giant twice as massive as all other planets combined. Its Great Red Spot is a giant storm larger than Earth that has raged for centuries.

Saturn

Adorned with a dazzling system of icy rings, Saturn is a gas giant with dozens of moons, including Titan, which has its own atmosphere and liquid lakes.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant that rotates on its side. It has a blue-green tint caused by methane in its atmosphere and is one of the coldest places in the solar system.

Neptune

Dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds, Neptune is the last of the hydrogen and helium gas giants. It was the first planet found via mathematical calculation.
